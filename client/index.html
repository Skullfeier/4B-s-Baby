What is bruteforce?

A brute-force attack is a fundamental technique used in cybersecurity to gain unauthorized access to a system. It involves systematically attempting every possible combination of characters to guess passwords or encryption keys until the correct one is found. Despite being a straightforward approach, brute-force attacks can be highly effective, particularly against weak passwords or inadequately protected systems. With the advent of high-performance computing, attackers can automate this process, making it faster and more efficient. This type of attack underscores the importance of robust security practices, such as using strong, complex passwords and implementing additional security measures like multi-factor authentication to safeguard sensitive information.
